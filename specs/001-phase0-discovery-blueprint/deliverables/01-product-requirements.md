# 01 — Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Platform Identity
The Nader George Educational Platform is a **comprehensive academic control system**, not merely a video hosting or content distribution site. It is designed to mirror the strict discipline, continuous assessment, and close follow-up of a premium physical tutoring center. Every feature serves the goal of academic excellence and accountability.

## Target Audience
1. **Students (Four Grades):** First, Second, and Third Secondary, plus Third Preparatory. They need clear paths, motivating progress, and strict boundaries.
2. **Parents:** Require transparent visibility into their child's attendance, performance, and behavior without needing deep technical knowledge.
3. **Assistants (Four Sub-roles):** Need efficient tools to manage thousands of students, grade homework, track attendance, and follow up with at-risk students.
4. **Admin (Nader George):** Needs absolute control over content, codes, assistant permissions, and macro-level platform analytics.

## Brand Identity
The platform's UX and voice must reflect the teacher's unique teaching style:
- **Youthful & Energetic:** Modern, fast, and engaging.
- **Simple:** No overwhelming menus; clear, guided paths (maximum 3 clicks to content).
- **Story-Based & Visual:** Heavy reliance on mind maps, tables, and narrative structures to explain complex concepts.
- **Motivating but Firm:** Encourages progress through gamification but enforces strict rules (e.g., cannot skip videos, must pass exams to proceed).

## Value Proposition
Unlike generic course platforms (e.g., Udemy or standard LMS), this system enforces an **Academic Flow**. Progress is gated by performance. Students cannot simply "buy and watch"; they must "enroll and pass." The platform actively monitors engagement and flags at-risk students for human assistant follow-up.

## Content Hierarchy
The platform organizes learning material into a strict hierarchy:
1. **Package (Course/Term Level):** The purchasable unit (e.g., "Third Secondary - First Term").
2. **Content Section (Module/Month Level):** The logical grouping of lessons. *(Internal note: Systematically referred to as "Content Section" everywhere; historically referred to as "months").*
3. **Lesson:** The core atomic unit of learning, containing:
   - Videos (Main explanation, homework solution, etc.)
   - Written Summary (PDF/Text)
   - Short Questions (In-video or post-video)
   - Homework (Scored assignment)
   - Short Quiz (Quick check)
   - Downloadable Files (Worksheets)
   - Mind Map (Visual summary)
   - Revision Block (Pre-exam review material)

## Academic Flow
The core loop a student experiences enforces discipline:
1. **Exam (Gate 1):** Student must pass a prerequisite or entry exam (if configured).
2. **Lesson (Learning):** Student watches the video (watch control prevents skipping).
3. **Homework (Gate 2):** Student completes the mandatory homework assignment.
4. **Review (Feedback):** Assistants grade essay questions; system grades MCQs.
5. **Progression:** Only after passing Gate 2 can the student unlock the next logical Lesson.
